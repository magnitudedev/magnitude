# Ideate

Explore option space, surface tradeoffs, and produce recommendations for a decision.

## Approach

Use this skill when the right approach is genuinely unclear and the decision warrants structured analysis. Ideation is not planning — it produces options and a recommendation, not an executable plan.

Frame the decision before comparing solutions. Generate multiple distinct approaches before converging, including non-obvious or hybrid options. Analyze each option concretely: how it works, value, complexity, risks, dependencies, failure modes. Compare on shared criteria to expose meaningful tradeoffs.

Identify which choices are reversible vs hard-to-reverse, and where uncertainty materially affects selection. Recommend a preferred option with explicit rationale. Explain why alternatives were not selected.

Close ideation when outputs support a concrete decision, then route the selected direction into planning or implementation.

## Delegation

When assigning a worker to ideate, share in your spawn message:

- The decision question to explore, with scope boundaries
- Constraints and preferences: technical, product, UX, timeline, risk tolerance, maintainability
- Relevant context: prior decisions, research findings, architecture constraints, known failure modes
- How the output will be used — decision meeting, planning input, architecture choice
- Non-negotiables and evaluation criteria

Expect back: multiple viable options described concretely; structured tradeoff analysis with pros, cons, risks, and uncertainty; a clear recommendation with decision logic grounded in constraints; explanation of why alternatives were not selected; and a workspace document suitable for decision-making.

Shallow comparisons need deeper analysis before they're decision-ready. Recommendations without constraint-grounded rationale are opinions.

## Quality Bar

- Multiple distinct options are explored with concrete descriptions.
- Tradeoffs are assessed honestly, including downsides, risks, and uncertainty.
- A clear recommendation is provided with explicit rationale.
- Analysis depth is sufficient to support an actual decision.
- A workspace document is produced and shared for downstream use.

## Skill Evolution

Update this skill when:
- The user has preferences about how decisions should be framed or presented.
- A type of tradeoff analysis is repeatedly valuable — make it explicit.
